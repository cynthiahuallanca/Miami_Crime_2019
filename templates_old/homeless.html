Miami Dade Crime Study (2015 to current) - June 2019

Crime Committed by Homeless Individuals

When analyzing the jail booking data, we found that almost a third was committed by homeless persons. Homelessness is on decline in Miami Dade in recent years. As of early 2019, there were 3628 homeless individuals or families in Miami Dade. 1008 of those are unsheltered and 2620 are sheltered. Thanks for Miami Dade Homeless Trust which is a county government office which reaches out to those sleeping on the streets to provide hot showers, indoor meals and find affordable housing for them. The goal of Miami Dade Homeless Trust is to prevent homelessness.

However, booking and jailing homeless individuals due to their public drinking or small offences is not the real solution in preventing crime. To put them in jail and when they return to society, they are still homeless and they are still aimless. Without a secured job, without a place of their own, they will likey repeat breaking the laws.

A homeless inmate costs taxpayers $230 per day. To arrest them for small offences is not the most cost-effective way to help them out of the streets and be law-abiding citizens. Link to Miami-Dade Homeless Trust
